Financial Market Malaysia: A Data-Driven Insight

Financial markets in Malaysia play a crucial role in the country's economy, influencing everything from investment decisions to international trade. These markets encompass various financial instruments such as currencies, equities, bonds, and commodities, with exchange rates serving as a key indicator of economic health. The movement of the Malaysian Ringgit against other global currencies reflects broader market trends, economic conditions, and geopolitical events. Understanding these dynamics is essential for identifying patterns in currency valuation, assessing economic stability, and making informed decisions. Through the exploration of exchange rate trends, the analysis seeks to shed light on the interconnectedness of Malaysia's financial landscape with global economic forces.

Monetary Aggregates: M1, M2, M3

This illustrates the steady growth of monetary aggregates from 2013 to 2024, highlighting the increasing contributions of components like Fixed Deposits and Demand Deposits to the overall money supply. This upward trend reflects rising economic activity and potential impacts of monetary policies over time. Additionally, the chart reveals the evolving composition of monetary components, offering insights into consumer saving behaviors and seasonal monetary patterns.

Interest Rate Trends in Malaysia

These showcases the trend of monthly and annually interest rates set by Bank Negara Malaysia over a specified period. This time-series analysis helps in understanding how the central bank's policies have influenced the financial environment. The data reveals key moments of rate adjustments, potentially reflecting responses to economic events such as inflation, recession, or global market changes. The visualization allows for the identification of periods of higher or lower interest rates, providing insights into how these fluctuations could have impacted borrowing costs, investments, and economic growth.

Currency in Circulation by Denomination

This illustrates the evolution of currency in circulation across different denominations in Malaysia over time. By using a streamgraph, it effectively highlights the changes in the value of various coins and notes, allowing for a clear understanding of currency trends. The chart reveals notable shifts in circulation, with certain denominations experiencing growth or decline at different periods. The layered structure of the visualization emphasizes the composition of the currency supply, providing insights into how economic factors or monetary policies may have influenced the demand for specific denominations.

Global Exchange Rates Against MYR

The map shows the average exchange rates of the Malaysian Ringgit (MYR) worldwide in 2024, with colors indicating the relative strength of currencies against 1 MYR. Regions in dark red have weaker currencies, requiring more local currency to equal 1 MYR, while lighter areas indicate stronger currency positions relative to the Ringgit. The visualization highlights significant disparities across continents, particularly in South America and parts of Africa, where exchange rates are highest, reflecting economic variations in currency valuation globally.
